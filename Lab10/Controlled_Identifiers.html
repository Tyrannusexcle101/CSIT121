Controlled-Identifiers

  • What is it?
  • Why is it Important?
  • How is it used?
  • Sources

A Controlled-Identifier is a document that contains cryptographic material. It lists service endpoints for the purposes of verifying cryptographic proofs. The proofs work by forming and interacting with the controller of an identifier. Controlled-Identifiers identify a subject and provide verification methods such as public keys.
